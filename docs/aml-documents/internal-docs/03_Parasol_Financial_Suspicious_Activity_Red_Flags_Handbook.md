# Parasol Financial Suspicious Activity Red Flags Handbook

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This handbook summarizes common red flags relevant to AML investigations at Parasol Financial. It is intended to support investigators and analysts in identifying patterns that may warrant closer review.

## Use of Red Flags

Red flags are indicators, not proof of wrongdoing. A single red flag may not justify escalation on its own. Investigators should evaluate the totality of the activity, customer profile, and supporting evidence.

## General Customer Behavior Red Flags

The customer cannot clearly explain the purpose of the account or gives inconsistent explanations over time. The customer changes stated business activity without a clear commercial reason. The customer is reluctant to provide ownership information or provides documentation that appears incomplete or inconsistent. Multiple accounts appear connected by common contact details, devices, or transaction behavior without a clear disclosed relationship.

## Account Activity Red Flags

There is a sudden increase in transaction volume with no clear business trigger. Funds are received and then moved out quickly with limited account retention. Activity is inconsistent with stated occupation, revenue, payroll size, or business purpose. Multiple cash or cash-equivalent transactions occur just below internal review thresholds. The account shows circular movement of funds among related or seemingly unrelated accounts.

## Small Business and Commercial Red Flags

The business receives payments from unrelated parties inconsistent with its sector. The business shows wire activity that does not align with known suppliers, customers, or geographic footprint. The account uses personal and business products interchangeably in a way that obscures source or purpose of funds. The business opens an account with modest expected activity and quickly begins sending or receiving large cross-border payments.

## Cross-Border Red Flags

Funds move through multiple jurisdictions in short succession. Payments involve intermediaries or counterparties with no clear relation to the customer’s business purpose. The customer begins sending wires to locations outside its historical pattern without documentation or explanation. Transaction narratives are vague, repetitive, or commercially implausible.

## Structuring and Funnel Indicators

Multiple deposits occur in different locations and are quickly consolidated. Cash activity appears fragmented across time, channels, or related accounts. Funds are deposited by multiple parties and transferred shortly afterward. The customer appears to use several accounts to separate incoming and outgoing activity without a clear economic rationale.

## Documentary and Behavioral Red Flags

Invoices, contracts, or explanations are generic, inconsistent, or submitted only after repeated requests. Supporting documents do not align with transaction dates, values, or counterparties. The customer becomes evasive when asked about high-risk activity or insists on urgency without a valid explanation.

## Investigative Reminder

When one or more red flags are present, investigators should identify which specific indicators were observed, what evidence was reviewed, and whether the indicators remain unexplained after reasonable investigation.
