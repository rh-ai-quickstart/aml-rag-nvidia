# Parasol Financial Sanctions and AML Coordination Guide

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This guide clarifies when a case should remain within AML, when it should be referred to sanctions compliance, and when both teams should coordinate.

## General Rule

AML reviews focus on suspicious movement of funds and inconsistent customer activity. Sanctions reviews focus on restricted parties, prohibited activity, blocked or rejected transactions, and jurisdiction-related restrictions. Some cases require both perspectives.

## Refer to Sanctions Compliance When

A screened party requires review, a payment was blocked or rejected, a transaction involves a jurisdiction or routing concern requiring sanctions interpretation, or the investigator cannot determine whether activity creates a sanctions risk.

## Joint Review Scenarios

A joint review is appropriate when suspicious transactional patterns overlap with possible sanctions exposure, when there are multiple alerts involving the same parties across both domains, or when legal and reputational risk is elevated.

## Documentation

When a case is handed to or shared with sanctions compliance, the investigator must summarize the factual AML concern and avoid making a sanctions determination unless authorized to do so.

## Decision Ownership

Each team is responsible for its formal determination. AML should not close a case solely because sanctions review is underway if suspicious activity concerns remain unresolved.
