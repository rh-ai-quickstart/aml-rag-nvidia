# Parasol Financial AML Quality Assurance Review Checklist

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This checklist supports supervisory and quality review of completed AML investigations.

## QA Review Areas

The reviewer should confirm that the alert trigger is clearly described, customer context is adequately summarized, relevant transaction activity was reviewed, internal guidance was consulted where appropriate, red flags were identified accurately, and the conclusion is supported by the evidence documented.

The reviewer should also confirm that the investigator considered whether the activity aligned with customer profile, whether prior alerts were relevant, whether escalation decisions followed the Escalation Matrix, and whether case notes are sufficiently clear for a third party to understand the rationale.

## Common Defects

Common issues include vague narratives, missing evidence references, overreliance on system text, failure to explain why activity is unusual or not unusual, incomplete use of customer profile information, and unsupported closures where serious red flags remain unresolved.

## QA Outcome Categories

Cases may be marked as acceptable, acceptable with coaching, or requiring rework. Rework is required when documentation gaps, unsupported conclusions, or missed escalation triggers materially affect the quality of the case.

## Coaching Standard

QA feedback should be specific and tied to documented expectations in the Playbook, SOPs, and Drafting Standards.
