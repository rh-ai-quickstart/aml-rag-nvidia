# Parasol Financial AML Escalation Matrix

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This matrix defines when AML cases must be escalated and to whom.

## Escalation Paths

Cases should be escalated to Senior AML Investigations when activity is materially unexplained, repeated, or complex enough to require advanced review.

Cases should be escalated to Sanctions Compliance when the activity involves screened names, concerning jurisdictional links, blocked or rejected payment indicators, or other facts suggesting sanctions exposure.

Cases should be escalated to Fraud Investigations when account takeover, impersonation, first-party fraud, or suspicious use of customer credentials may explain the activity.

Cases should be escalated to Legal and Compliance Advisory when law enforcement requests are received, a matter may involve legal privilege or litigation hold, or the case presents heightened reputational risk.

Cases should be escalated to the SAR Review Committee when the investigator and manager conclude the activity may warrant suspicious activity reporting.

## Escalation Triggers

Escalation is mandatory when any of the following are present: material unexplained cross-border movement of funds, repeated alert generation with worsening patterns, unusual activity in newly opened accounts, use of multiple linked accounts to move funds rapidly, inability to obtain or validate a reasonable explanation, credible overlap with sanctions concerns, significant negative information tied to the activity, or any case where required internal thresholds are met.

## Documentation Requirement

Every escalation must include a concise handoff note explaining what triggered the escalation, what evidence has already been reviewed, what open questions remain, and what action is being requested from the receiving team.

## Manager Notification

Priority 1 cases require immediate manager awareness. Priority 2 cases require manager awareness if unresolved after initial review or if additional serious red flags emerge.
