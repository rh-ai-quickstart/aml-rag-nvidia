# Parasol Financial Politically Exposed Persons Review Procedure

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This procedure defines how AML investigators should handle cases involving politically exposed persons, related parties, or entities subject to enhanced due diligence because of public office exposure.

## Principle

PEP status does not mean activity is suspicious. It does require careful review because the relationship may present increased corruption, bribery, or misuse-of-funds risk.

## Review Steps

When a case involves a PEP or related entity, the investigator should confirm whether the relationship was identified during onboarding or periodic review, whether enhanced due diligence is current, and whether the account activity is consistent with stated source of wealth and source of funds information.

The investigator should evaluate whether account usage, incoming funds, outgoing payments, and counterparties align with the customer profile. If payments involve state-linked entities, intermediaries, shell-like structures, or unexplained cross-border transfers, the case should be reviewed carefully for escalation.

## Escalation

Escalate to senior AML review when PEP-related activity is materially unusual, involves opaque ownership or routing, or cannot be reasonably explained through available documentation.

## Documentation

Case notes must identify the PEP connection, summarize current due diligence status, and explain whether the activity is consistent with the information the bank maintains about the relationship.
