# Parasol Financial Cross-Border Wire Review Procedure

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This procedure defines how investigators review alerts involving international wires and other cross-border payment activity.

## Objective

The goal is to determine whether the payment activity is consistent with the customer’s known profile, business purpose, and historical behavior, or whether it shows unexplained patterns that warrant escalation.

## Review Steps

First, review the customer profile, expected geography, transaction history, and products used. Determine whether the customer has a known reason to send or receive cross-border payments. For business customers, compare the activity with known suppliers, customers, shipment corridors, and business footprint.

Second, review the payment pattern. Consider frequency, value, timing, destination, origin, counterparties, and any rapid onward movement. Identify whether payments are concentrated in new geographies, split across multiple beneficiaries, routed through unusual paths, or inconsistent with historical business behavior.

Third, review supporting materials already on file. These may include account opening documents, expected activity descriptions, relationship manager notes, and submitted invoices or contracts where applicable.

Fourth, assess whether the activity has a reasonable and documented business explanation. If it does not, determine whether the facts support escalation for enhanced investigation, sanctions coordination, or SAR consideration.

## Common Escalation Indicators

New accounts engaging in substantial cross-border activity shortly after opening, repeated transfers to unrelated counterparties, vague payment descriptions, payment corridors inconsistent with customer profile, and rapid in-and-out movement with limited economic retention.

## Case Note Requirement

The investigator must state whether the customer’s expected business or personal profile supports the geography and purpose of the wires reviewed.
