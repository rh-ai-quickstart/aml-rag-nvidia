# Parasol Financial AML Investigation Playbook

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This playbook defines the standard investigation process for anti-money laundering alerts and cases at Parasol Financial. It is intended to help investigators review suspicious activity consistently, document findings clearly, and escalate matters appropriately.

## Scope

This playbook applies to alerts generated from transaction monitoring, sanctions-adjacent reviews referred to AML, customer due diligence reviews referred for investigation, manual referrals from fraud or branch operations, and periodic reviews of high-risk customers.

## Investigation Objective

The objective of an AML investigation is to determine whether observed activity is reasonably explained by the customer profile, stated business purpose, and expected account behavior, or whether the activity warrants escalation, enhanced review, or suspicious activity reporting.

## Standard Investigation Workflow

When an alert is assigned, the investigator first reviews the alert narrative, rule trigger, and underlying transaction data. The investigator then gathers customer context, including account tenure, products used, customer risk rating, business type if applicable, ownership data, prior alerts, prior case outcomes, and any known compliance concerns.

The investigator next reviews the activity in context. This includes the size, frequency, origin, destination, counterparties, channels used, geographic pattern, and any change from historical behavior. The investigator should consider whether the activity is unusual for the customer, inconsistent with the stated purpose of the account, structured in a suspicious way, or connected to known risk indicators.

The investigator must then consult relevant internal guidance, including the Alert Triage SOP, Customer Risk Rating Methodology, Cross-Border Wire Review Procedure, Suspicious Activity Red Flags Handbook, and any applicable sanctions coordination guidance. If the customer is a politically exposed person, cash-intensive business, nonprofit with cross-border exposure, money services business, or import/export entity, the investigator should also review any enhanced due diligence requirements.

The investigator documents findings in the case management system using the approved Case Notes Template. Notes must identify what evidence was reviewed, what patterns were observed, what explanations were considered, and why the case is being closed, escalated, or recommended for suspicious activity reporting.

## Required Evidence Review

At a minimum, investigators should review customer profile and KYC data, related accounts where available, recent transaction history, prior alerts and investigations, supporting documentation already on file, internal policy guidance relevant to the alert type, and any applicable negative news or sanctions screening results already captured by the bank’s approved tools.

## Disposition Options

An investigator may close the case as explained when the activity is supported by a reasonable and documented business or customer explanation consistent with available evidence. An investigator may close the case as inconclusive but non-escalated when suspicious indicators are limited and the totality of information does not support further action. An investigator must escalate the case when the activity involves significant unexplained movement of funds, unusual use of multiple accounts, apparent funnel behavior, rapid movement across borders, layering indicators, sanctions concerns, adverse media with a strong nexus to the activity, or repeated alerts without a credible explanation.

## Escalation Expectations

Escalation is required when the investigator cannot explain material activity through documented evidence, when the activity may involve sanctions exposure, when the customer risk profile materially changes, or when internal thresholds in the Escalation Matrix are met. Escalations may go to senior AML investigations, sanctions compliance, fraud investigations, legal, or the SAR review committee depending on the facts.

## Documentation Standard

A case should be understandable to a reviewer who has not previously seen it. Case notes should be factual, concise, and supported by evidence. Investigators should avoid speculation, legal conclusions, or unsupported assumptions. If a conclusion depends on missing information, that gap should be stated explicitly.

## Timeliness

Investigations should be completed within internal service-level expectations based on alert priority. High-priority alerts must be reviewed promptly and escalated without delay if risk indicators are significant. Investigators must remain aware of applicable regulatory reporting timeframes and internal escalation deadlines.
