# Parasol Financial Customer Risk Rating Methodology

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This methodology explains how Parasol Financial assigns customer AML risk ratings. The rating helps determine the level of monitoring, review intensity, and enhanced due diligence required.

## Risk Rating Categories

Customers are assigned one of three overall risk tiers: Low, Medium, or High. The rating is based on the combination of customer type, products used, geography, transaction behavior, ownership transparency, and negative risk indicators.

## Core Risk Factors

Customer type matters because some sectors and legal structures present greater opacity or transaction complexity. Products matter because certain channels, such as international wires or cash-intensive services, may create higher exposure. Geography matters when customers, beneficiaries, counterparties, or business operations involve jurisdictions subject to heightened internal review. Ownership transparency matters because layered ownership or unclear beneficial control increases risk. Behavioral indicators matter because unusual or rapidly changing activity may elevate risk regardless of onboarding profile.

## Low-Risk Characteristics

Low-risk customers generally have transparent ownership, straightforward account purpose, stable domestic transaction patterns, limited cash exposure, and no meaningful history of unresolved compliance issues.

## Medium-Risk Characteristics

Medium-risk customers may have moderate cross-border exposure, business complexity, elevated transaction volume, sector-specific risk factors, or prior alerts that were resolved but warrant continued monitoring.

## High-Risk Characteristics

High-risk customers may include entities with complex ownership, significant cross-border payment activity, activity involving internally designated high-risk jurisdictions, customers in sectors requiring enhanced diligence, customers with repeated unexplained alerts, or relationships involving politically exposed persons or other high-risk control factors.

## Rating Adjustments

A customer’s rating may be adjusted upward when activity materially diverges from stated purpose, when documentation is incomplete or outdated, or when adverse risk information emerges. A rating may be adjusted downward only when updated due diligence and sustained monitoring support a lower-risk conclusion.

## Use in Investigations

Risk rating informs triage and investigative intensity but does not predetermine the outcome of a case. A low-risk customer may still generate a serious case if the activity is significant and unexplained. A high-risk rating does not by itself mean activity is suspicious.

## Review Frequency

High-risk customers must be reviewed more frequently than lower-risk customers. Significant changes in ownership, geography, product use, or alert history should trigger reassessment.
