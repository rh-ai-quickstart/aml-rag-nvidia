# Parasol Financial Guidance for Activity Involving High-Risk Jurisdictions

Parasol Financial

## Purpose

This document explains how investigators should handle activity involving jurisdictions designated for heightened internal review.

## Guiding Principle

Jurisdictional exposure is a risk factor, not an automatic basis for escalation. Investigators must consider the nature of the relationship, the transaction purpose, the customer profile, and any supporting evidence.

## What Counts as Jurisdictional Exposure

Exposure may include customer residence, business operations, counterparties, beneficiaries, originators, intermediaries, shipment locations, payment corridors, or control persons associated with a designated jurisdiction.

## Investigation Expectations

When jurisdictional exposure is present, the investigator should review whether the customer disclosed this connection at onboarding or during periodic review, whether the activity aligns with stated business purpose, whether the payment route makes commercial sense, and whether documentation supports the transaction.

The investigator should also determine whether sanctions-related review is required under the Sanctions and AML Coordination Guide. If there is uncertainty about whether the issue is purely AML or also sanctions-related, the matter should be discussed promptly with sanctions compliance.

## Documentation Standard

Case notes must describe the nature of the jurisdictional link and explain why it did or did not increase concern in the context of the overall activity.

## Enhanced Review Scenarios

Enhanced review is expected when the jurisdictional link is new, inconsistent with customer profile, combined with rapid movement of funds, tied to layered routing, or accompanied by poor documentation or evasive explanations.
